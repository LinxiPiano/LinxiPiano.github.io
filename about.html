    Linxi Zhao is a Chinese collaborative pianist and educator currently serving on the faculty at William & Mary. A passionate advocate for the intersection of music and technology, she explores creative synergies between classical performance and emerging digital tools, fostering curiosity and innovation in her teaching and artistry.

    Linxis collaborative portfolio spans opera, choral arts, chamber music, and jazz. In 2019, supported by the Wuhan Conservatory of Music, she curated and performed the concert series "Melodic Mastery: Unveiling the Artistry in Chinese Art Songs," touring major institutions including Hubei, Guangzhou, and China Three Gorges Universities. Her notable performances also include the Yangtze Music Festival and the Hubei Changtian Tower series. As a soloist, her recitals have been broadcast on Huangshi TV and Wuhan Media Group, and she has been a prize-winner in the Collegiate Artists Competition.

     

    Her research focuses on underrepresented American art song composers (such as Paul Schoenfield and Ned Rorem) and the integration of AI in music. Recent projects include the lecture-demonstration "Can AI Rock?" and the workshop "Improvising through the Circle of Fifths." She is currently preparing a recording project, Teaching Music Selection of Chinese Piano Works (Huangshi Musicians Association Press), which analyzes contemporary piano techniques in Chinese repertoire.

     

    Linxi previously served as an Adjunct Instructor at North Carolina Central University, specializing in vocal diction for German, French, and Italian. She has presented her work at the Music Teachers National Association (MTNA) and the International Keyboard Collaborative Arts Society (IKCAS) CollabFest.

    She holds a Doctor of Musical Arts (DMA) in Collaborative Piano from the University of North Carolina at Greensboro, as well as a Master and Bachelor of Music from the Wuhan Conservatory of Music. Her primary mentors include Qionggang Ruan, Congcong Chai, and James Douglass. Originally trained as a vocalist before transitioning to jazz and piano, Linxi brings a unique multi-disciplinary perspective to her work. Outside of music, she enjoys tennis and cooking.
